## Overview

AirPlay Receiver lets your Mac act as a target for media or screen sharing from another Apple device. Instead of only sending content outward, the Mac becomes the place another device can stream into.

## How to use it

1. Enable AirPlay Receiver in the relevant system settings.
2. Make sure the Mac is available on the same general Apple ecosystem conditions the feature expects.
3. Start an AirPlay session from the sending device.
4. Confirm that the Mac appears as a receiver and behaves the way you expected.

## Why it matters

This is useful when the Mac itself is the screen or speaker you want to use, especially in Apple-heavy setups where an iPhone, iPad, and Mac regularly hand media or display duties back and forth.

## Caveats

- Nearby-device features depend on account, network, and compatibility conditions.
- If the Mac is not showing up as a receiver, the problem may be discovery rather than the setting itself.
- It is a convenience feature, not a universal answer for every casting setup.
