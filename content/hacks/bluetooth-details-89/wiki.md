## Overview

Holding `Shift + Option` while clicking the Bluetooth menu exposes a more technical view than the normal consumer-friendly menu. Depending on the device and macOS version, you may see connection state, address information, battery reporting, or codec and signal details for audio devices.

## How to use it

1. Make sure the Bluetooth icon is visible in the menu bar or Control Center.
2. Hold `Shift + Option`.
3. Click the Bluetooth control while keeping those keys pressed.
4. Read the expanded details for the connected device or menu item you care about.

## Why it matters

This is useful when Bluetooth "kind of works" but not well. If headphones sound unstable, a mouse lags, or a keyboard drops connection intermittently, the standard menu is usually too shallow to help. The advanced menu gives you a faster first look before you move on to deeper troubleshooting.

## Caveats

- The exact fields vary by macOS release and by hardware.
- This is an inspection tool, not a fix by itself.
- If the menu is missing, first make the Bluetooth control visible instead of assuming the shortcut failed.
