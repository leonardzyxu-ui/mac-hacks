## Overview

`Cmd + ,` opens the Settings or Preferences window in many Mac apps. It is not a universal operating-system command, but it is common enough across Mac software that it is worth treating as a strong convention.

## How to use it

1. Focus the app whose settings you want.
2. Press `Cmd + ,`.
3. If the app supports the convention, its settings window will open immediately.
4. Adjust what you need and close the settings window when done.

## Why it matters

This shortcut is good because it saves repeated menu hunting. Once you learn it, you can jump straight to app preferences for browsers, editors, note apps, and many other Mac-native tools without opening the app menu first.

## Caveats

- Some apps do not support it.
- Web wrappers and cross-platform apps are less consistent than native Mac apps.
- If nothing happens, check the app menu to see whether the app uses a different settings command.
