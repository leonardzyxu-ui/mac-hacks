## Overview

`Cmd + Space` opens Spotlight, which is part launcher, part search box, and part quick command line for everyday Mac tasks. It is often the fastest way to open an app, find a file, perform a simple calculation, or jump into a search without digging through folders or menus.

## How to use it

1. Press `Cmd + Space`.
2. Start typing the app, file, contact, calculation, or query you want.
3. Use the arrow keys if multiple results appear.
4. Press Return to open the highlighted result.

## Why it matters

Spotlight is one of the highest-leverage shortcuts on macOS because it replaces a lot of low-value navigation. It is faster than opening Launchpad for most apps, faster than browsing through Applications, and often good enough for quick file lookup.

## Caveats

- Results depend on Spotlight indexing, so newly changed files may not appear instantly.
- It is best treated as a launcher and locator, not a perfect full-database search engine.
- If another app has taken over `Cmd + Space`, check keyboard shortcut conflicts.
