## Overview

Three Finger Drag lets you move windows and other draggable items by sliding three fingers on the trackpad instead of clicking and holding. Many long-time Mac users swear by it because it makes window movement feel lighter and less physically awkward.

## How to use it

1. Enable Three Finger Drag in the relevant Accessibility or Trackpad settings.
2. Place three fingers on the trackpad over the draggable item.
3. Move your fingers to drag it.
4. Lift to release.

## Why it matters

This feature reduces strain and makes drag-heavy workflows feel smoother. It is especially good for people who dislike click-and-hold gestures or who spend a lot of time arranging windows and moving items.

## Caveats

- It changes muscle memory, so the first few days can feel strange.
- Some other trackpad gestures may feel different once it is enabled.
- It is a preference feature; if you hate it, turning it back off is a completely reasonable choice.
